Career Path Exploration

Early high school students would benefit from a system that can motivate and engage them to identify, explore, and evaluate their options after high school. This system aims to increase students’ confidence in their next step and to decrease the amount of students who enroll in colleges undecided.

The Challenge

Changing the
Future Workforce

Our clients approached us with the challenge of connecting the emergent workforce to the high-demand jobs of the future. We found that meeting young people where they are — as high school students — could be a central opportunity for this project.

Our work aims to address this issue through original human-centered research and design with the end goal of creating a product and/or service that aids career planning for students.

Our Model

To help students feel confident in a career direction, we created The Career Discovery Loop model. The model that visualizes the three stages we observed from interviewing successful stories of working adults’ journeys. The three phases of loop are listed below.

Career Discovery Loop

We generated a model to describe the ideal process of career discovery for students. We found this pattern to be most effective according to our interviews with students and working professionals.

Identify

Encourages the student to articulate their interest and helps match their interests to potential career options. In the end, students should be able to decide on a direction they’d like to explore.

Explore

Provides the student with experiences so they can understand if a certain career direction is right for them or not. Experiences can be simple and quick, or longer engagements.

Evaluate

Requires the student to reflect on their experience and articulate their likes and dislikes. This helps the student either course correct to explore another career option or focus in on a career path they feel confident.
